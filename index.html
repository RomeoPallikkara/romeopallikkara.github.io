About Me

Who I Am

Passionate about understanding the cosmos through data and observation

üåå

I am a PhD student in Astronomy at INAF - Osservatorio Astronomico di Trieste, Italy, dedicated to unraveling the mysteries of the early universe through cutting-edge observational techniques.

My research focuses on studying the evolution of galaxies and supermassive black holes in the early universe using data from premier space telescopes including the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) and the Hubble Space Telescope (HST).

I completed my BS-MS in Physics from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Mohali, where I developed expertise in observational astronomy, spectroscopic analysis, and computational astrophysics.

My work combines theoretical understanding with practical data analysis, utilizing advanced photometry, spectral energy distribution modeling, and statistical techniques to push the boundaries of our understanding of cosmic evolution.
